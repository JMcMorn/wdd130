Lindisfarne - England.

Back when I was a lad we lived perhaps 15 minutes from here. I suppose "City" is a bit of a stretch, it's a ruined monastary on a small island, home to mostly puffins and seagulls. It's still one of my favourite places with a rich history. It's where the vikings first landed when they invaded England. One of the kinds of Engalnd attempted to flea there and was swallowd by the tides. A rather unique aspect to it is that the island is only accessible at low tide. There are signs all along the road warning that if the tide is about to turn to stay away. There's very tall towers so the foolish needn't perish but there are many vantage points from the isle to watch car's get washed away. There's always at least one.

I suppose it's my favourite aside from all the cool history is because it's home to many of my strongest memories from my youth there. Swimming in the North Sea (ill advised), lobster picking, watching the turning of the tides and eating soft serve in the pouring rain were all unforgettable.
